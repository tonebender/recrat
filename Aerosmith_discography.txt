American [[rock music|rock]] band [[Aerosmith]] has released 15 [[studio album]]s, nine [[live album]]s, 15 [[compilation album]]s, two [[extended play]]s, and 72 [[single (music)|singles]]. Aerosmith was formed in [[Boston|Boston, Massachusetts]] in 1970 by vocalist [[Steven Tyler]], guitarists [[Joe Perry (musician)|Joe Perry]] and [[Ray Tabano]], bassist [[Tom Hamilton (musician)|Tom Hamilton]], and drummer [[Joey Kramer]]. Tabano was replaced by [[Brad Whitford]] in 1971. Other than a period from 1979 to 1984, this lineup has remained the same.<ref name="Allmusic bio">{{cite web | url={{Allmusic|class=artist|id=p3508|pure_url=yes}} | title=AllMusic | website=AllMusic | access-date=2008-06-24 }}</ref>

Twenty-one of Aerosmith's songs have reached the top 40 of the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] and the band has long been a stalwart of the [[Mainstream Rock (chart)|Mainstream Rock Tracks]] chart, achieving nine number-one hits on that chart to date. An additional 28 of the band's songs have reached the top 40 on various charts worldwide. The band has achieved four multi-platinum singles ("[[Dream On (Aerosmith song)|Dream On]]", "[[Sweet Emotion]]", "[[Walk This Way]]", and "[[I Don't Want to Miss a Thing]]"), while an additional eight singles have attained silver, gold, or platinum certifications in various territories.

Aerosmith is estimated to have sold well over 150 million albums around the world, making them the biggest-selling hard rock band in United States history.<ref>{{cite news |last=McShane |first=Larry |title=New Aerosmith 'Guitar Hero' game |work=New York Daily News |url=http://www.nydailynews.com/entertainment/music/2008/02/16/2008-02-16_new_aerosmith_guitar_hero_game.html |access-date=March 31, 2008 |date=February 15, 2008 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080412025546/http://www.nydailynews.com/entertainment/music/2008/02/16/2008-02-16_new_aerosmith_guitar_hero_game.html |archive-date=12 April 2008  |url-status=live}}</ref> As of November 2021, the band has sold 69.5 million albums in the United States in terms of certification units,<ref name="RIAA&nbsp;— Top Selling Artists">{{cite web| title=Top Selling Artists | publisher=[[Recording Industry Association of America]] (RIAA.com)| url=https://www.riaa.com/goldandplatinum.php?content_selector=top-selling-artists }}</ref> and 31,702,000 albums since 1991 when [[SoundScan]] started tracking actual sales figures.<ref name="US Sales">{{cite web|url=http://music.yahoo.com/blogs/chart-watch/week-ending-july-28-2013-albums-finally-woman-152221250.html |title=Week Ending July 28, 2013. ===Notes===
*The Mainstream Rock Tracks chart was not created until 1981 and the Top 40 Mainstream chart was not created until the 1990s. Thus, both charts only include material after their creation. 
*"Crazy" was released as a double A-side with "Blind Man" in the United Kingdom, so the chart position of #23 is given for both.

==Peak positions on other charts==
Additionally, the band charted these on other charts:
*"[[Dude (Looks Like a Lady)]]" (1988) – #41 [[Hot Dance Club Songs|Dance Music/Club Play]]
*"[[I Don't Want to Miss a Thing]]" (1998) – #13 [[Adult Contemporary (chart)|Adult Contemporary]], #14 [[Hot Latin Songs|Hot Latin Tracks]]
*"[[Jaded (Aerosmith song)|Jaded]]" (2001) – #8 [[Top 40 Tracks]], #21 [[Tropical Songs|Latin Tropical/Salsa Airplay]], #30 [[Latin Pop Airplay]]
*"[[Fly Away from Here]]" (2001) – #34 Top 40 Tracks
*"[[Legendary Child]]" (2012) – #19 [[Active rock|Active Rock]], #3 [[Heritage rock|Heritage Rock]], #27 [[List of number-one Billboard Rock Songs|Hot Rock Songs]], #69 [[Canadian Hot 100]] Airplay
*"[[Lover Alot]]" (2012) – #19 Heritage Rock, #47 Hot Rock Songs
*"[[What Could Have Been Love]]" (2012) – #28 Adult Contemporary, #48 Hot Rock Songs
